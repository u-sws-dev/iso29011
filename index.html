Maturity Assessment Protocols
ISO 29010 is a management system standard that establishes a discipline agnostic framework for owner/operators of assests/facilities to establish management systems consistent with their context, business and operational objectives, ISO 29011 establishes a methodology and criteria for assessing an organisation’s level of conformance and maturity to the ISO 290110 requirements.
This assessment tool is designed to record and analyse the findings of assessments to the ISO 29010 requirements benchmarked against the ISO 29011 maturity criteria.
User Cases.
The assessment tool is intended to have the scalability to record and analyse assessments of;
  1. Single current state assessment of a defined organisation/scope
  2. Current state assessment and future state vision comparision including identification of actions proposed to achieve the desired future state (two assessment records)
  3. Multiple assessments capturing changes in maturity over time
  4. Multiple assessments of differnt segments of an organisation to benchmark performance and to identify and leverage practices as the basis for organisation wide improvement
  5. Multiple assesssments benchmarking peer organisations and to identify and leverage best practice for broader segment or community benefit
  6. Single current state assessment of a defined organisatio/scope
The scope and purpose (user story) for use of the the assessment tool is defined in the Assessment Terms of Reference.
Maturity Levels.
Maturity levels are defined as :
  1. Awareness: Management demonstrates understanding of and commitment to addressing the requirement
  2. Approach: Defined plans and processes meet the requirement
  3. Deployment: Plan/process effectively implemented including baseline correction/corrective action/continuous improvement (Clause 10.2)
  4. Improving: Sustained improvement with demonstration of value realised/protected by the improvements
  5. Transforming: Demonstration that the OMS is a strategic enabler of step changes in the operations and operational performance including engagement with and contributions to stakeholders
Assessment Methodology.
  1. Plan assessment
    • Purpose
    • Sponsor
    • Objectives
    • Backgroup/risks/opportunities
    • Team
    • Date and location
    • Methodology and reporting
    • List of interviewees and key documents
  2. Conduct assessment
  3. Determine and record maturity scores
  4. Assessment reporting and feedback

4.1 Understanding the organization and its context
The organization shall determine external and internal issues that are relevant to its purpose and that affect its ability to achieve the intended result(s) of its operating management system. When determining the external and internal issues, the organization shall consider:
  • the context of the sector in which it operates
  • the context of the value chain, both external and internal
  • the lifecycle of the organization’s operation(s)
  • its license to operate.
  • the sustainability of the communities in which it operates
  • whether climate change is a relevant issue
NOTE 1 Business objectives focus on opportunity before risk and describe what the organization wants to achieve, which can be part of the organization’s vision, mission and strategic directions. NOTE 2 Understanding the internal context can be facilitated by considering issues related to purpose, values, culture, knowledge and performance of the organization. NOTE 3 Understanding the external context can be facilitated by considering issues arising from legal, technological, competitive, market, cultural, social and economic environments, whether international, national, regional or local.

Context description including relevant external and internal issues are analysed, understood and available to inform the scope and design of the OMS. 4.4 Operating management system
The organization shall establish, implement, maintain and continually improve an operating management system, including the processes needed and their interactions, in accordance with the requirements of this document. The organization shall determine the processes needed for the operating management system and their application throughout the organization. The organization shall:
  • determine the inputs required and the outputs expected from these processes
  • determine the sequence and interaction of these processes
  • determine and apply the criteria and methods (including monitoring, measurements and related performance indicators) needed to ensure the effective operation and control of these processes
  • determine the resources needed for these processes and ensure their availability (ref 7.1, 7.2, 7.3)
  • assign the responsibilities and authorities for these processes
  • address the risks and opportunities
  • evaluate process suitability and effectiveness and implement improvements to ensure achievement of intended results
Documented information shall be available to support the operation of its processes and to have confidence that the processes are being carried out as planned. The organization shall define the extent of documented information required to meet relevant stakeholder requirements (see 4.2 and 4.3).

5.1 Leadership and commitment
Top management shall demonstrate leadership and commitment with respect to the operating management system by:
  • ensuring that the organization’s policy and objectives are established and are consistent with the strategic direction of the organization
  • ensuring the integration of the operating management system requirements into the organization’s business processes
  • ensuring that the resources needed for the operating management system are available
  • communicating the importance of effective operating management and of conforming to the operating management system requirements
  • ensuring that the operating management system achieves its intended result(s)
  • directing and supporting persons to contribute to the effectiveness of the operating management system
  • promoting continual improvement
  • supporting other relevant roles to demonstrate their leadership as it applies to their areas of responsibility
NOTE Reference to “business” in this context can be interpreted broadly to mean those activities that are core to the purposes of the organization’s existence. Top management shall demonstrate leadership and commitment with respect to license to operate by ensuring that:
  • the risks and opportunities that can affect license to operate and the ability to enhance stakeholder satisfaction are determined and addressed
  • applicable statutory, regulatory and sustainability requirements are determined, understood and met
  • stakeholder satisfaction is enhanced

  • give assurance that the operating management system can achieve its intended result(s)
  • prevent, or reduce, undesired effects
  • achieve improvement.

  • actions to address these reisks and opportunities including;
    • defining techniques, tools and their application for risk identification, assessment, prevention and mitigation

    • evaluating risk and develop risk controls
    • applying appropriate risk treatments
  • how to
    • integrate and implement the actions into its operating management system
    • evaluate the effectiveness of these actions

6.2 Objectives and planning to achieve them

  • be consistent with the operating policy
  • consider risks and opportunities
  • be measurable
  • consider applicable requirements
  • be monitored
  • be communicated to relevant stakeholders
  • be reviewed and updated as appropriate
  • be available as documented information
When establishing its objectives, the organization shall consider the requirements of relevant stakeholders and of other financial, technical, legal, regulatory, sustainability and organizational requirements in the operating management planning process. When planning how to achieve its objectives, the organization shall determine and document:
  • what will be done
  • what resources will be required
  • who will be responsible
  • when it will be completed
  • how the results will be evaluated
When planning how to achieve its objectives, the organization shall define:
  • method and criteria for decision making and prioritizing of the activities
  • processes and methods to be employed in managing assets over their lifecycles
  • appropriate planning time horizon(s)
  • technical, non-technical and financial implications
  • sustainability implications
  • review period.

6.3 Planning of changes

  • Products and services
The organization shall assess risks and opportunities associated with planned or unplanned changes, permanent or temporary, that can have an impact on achieving the objectives. 10.3 Organizational knowledge
The organization shall determine the knowledge necessary for the operation of its processes and to achieve business objectives. This knowledge shall be maintained and be made available to the extent necessary. When addressing changing needs and trends, the organization shall consider its current knowledge and determine how to acquire or access any necessary additional knowledge and required updates. NOTE 1 Organizational knowledge is knowledge specific to the organization; it is generally gained by experience. It is information that is used and shared to achieve the organization’s objectives. NOTE 2 Organizational knowledge can be based on:
  • internal sources (e.g. intellectual property; knowledge gained from experience; lessons learned from failures and successful projects; capturing and sharing undocumented knowledge and experience; the results of improvements in processes, products and services);
  • external sources (e.g. standards; academia; conferences; gathering knowledge from customers or external providers).
